Differential Equations

Ordinary differential equations will not be covered in this chapter as they are an earlier topic. However, I will do a brief definition at the start. PDEs will be the primary focus in this chapter.

ODEs and PDEs

Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) are differential equations that have a single variable. ODEs have a general form:

$$F\left(x,y,\frac{dy}{dx},\frac{d^{2}y}{dx^{2}},...\right)=0$$

where:

  • x is the independent variable
  • y is the dependent variable

Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) involve multiple independent variables. Instead of using standard derivatives (d), they use partial derivatives (âˆ‚) to show the change with respect to multiple variables. The general form is:

$$F\left(x,y,u,\frac{\partial u}{\partial x},\frac{\partial u}{\partial y},...\right)=0$$

where:

  • x, y are independent variables
  • u(x, y) is the dependent variable

Fundamentally, an ODE can be seen as a way to track a single car, while a PDE tracks all the traffic in a city.

Types of ODEs

ODEs are classified by their order (the highest derivative present) and whether they are linear or non-linear.

First-order ODE:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} + y = x$$

Second-order ODE:

$$\frac{d^{2}y}{dx^{2}}+2\frac{dy}{dx}+y=0$$

Higher-order ODE:

$$a_{n}(x)\frac{d^{n}y}{dx^{n}} + a_{n-1}(x)\frac{d^{n-1}y}{dx^{n-1}} + ... + a_{1}(x)\frac{dy}{dx} + a_{0}(x)y = f(x)$$

A linear ODE is one where the dependent variable and its derivatives appear only in a linear form (i.e., they are not multiplied together). Anything else is considered non-linear.

Solving ODEs

Separable ODEs:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = f(x)g(y)$$

Rearranges to:

$$\frac{dy}{g(y)} = f(x)dx$$

Integrating both sides:

$$\int \frac{dy}{g(y)} = \int f(x)dx$$

Integrating Factor Method:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = Q(x)$$

Multiplying by the integrating factor:

$$\mu(x) = e^{\int P(x)dx}$$

Gives:

$$\frac{d}{dx}(\mu(x)y) = \mu(x)Q(x)$$

Solving:

$$y = \frac{1}{\mu(x)} \int \mu(x)Q(x)dx + C$$

Higher-Order Equations

Homogeneous Equations with Constant Coefficients:

$$a_n \frac{d^n y}{dx^n} + a_{n-1} \frac{d^{n-1} y}{dx^{n-1}} + ... + a_1 \frac{dy}{dx} + a_0 y = 0$$

Non-Homogeneous Equations:

$$a_n \frac{d^n y}{dx^n} + a_{n-1} \frac{d^{n-1} y}{dx^{n-1}} + ... + a_1 \frac{dy}{dx} + a_0 y = f(x)$$
